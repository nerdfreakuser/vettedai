Speed: NordVPN wins

NordVPN's custom NordLynx protocol — built on WireGuard with a proprietary double-NAT layer — consistently outperforms Surfshark's standard WireGuard implementation in our tests. On a 500 Mbps connection, NordVPN retained around 480 Mbps while Surfshark averaged around 420 Mbps. Both are more than fast enough for streaming and gaming, but NordVPN's advantage matters on slower connections or at distance.

Surfshark's speeds are still excellent and well above what most users need. For casual browsing, streaming, or working remotely, you won't notice the difference.

Price: Surfshark wins

Surfshark is one of the cheapest premium VPNs on the market, and it undercuts NordVPN significantly on 2-year plans. If you're budget-sensitive, that's a meaningful difference over time.

Privacy & Security: Tie

Both NordVPN and Surfshark have been independently audited for their no-logs policies — NordVPN by PwC, Surfshark by Deloitte. Both use RAM-only servers so nothing persists on disk. Both offer AES-256 encryption and DNS leak protection.

NordVPN adds Threat Protection Pro (malware blocking even when VPN is off) which is a meaningful extra. Surfshark counters with Nexus — a rotating IP feature that makes your traffic even harder to trace. It's genuinely a tie on security fundamentals.

Streaming: NordVPN edges ahead

NordVPN's larger server network gives it a consistent edge for unblocking streaming services. In our tests, NordVPN accessed Netflix US, BBC iPlayer, Disney+, Hulu, and Amazon Prime reliably every time. Surfshark also unblocks most services, but we found occasional issues with BBC iPlayer on some server locations.

For most users, both will work fine. NordVPN is the safer choice for heavy streamers who need reliable access.

Device Connections: Surfshark wins

Surfshark allows unlimited simultaneous connections — cover every device in your home on a single plan. NordVPN caps at 10, which is enough for most individuals but could be limiting for larger households or teams. If you want to share with family or protect many devices, Surfshark is the better call here.
