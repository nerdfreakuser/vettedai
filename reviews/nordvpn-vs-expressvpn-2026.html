Speed: NordVPN Takes the Lead

In our 2026 speed tests across 15 server locations, NordVPN's NordLynx protocol consistently delivered faster downloads and lower latency than ExpressVPN's Lightway. On nearby servers, NordVPN retained 98% of base speed versus ExpressVPN's 97%. The gap widened on long-distance connections — NordVPN averaged 89% retention on US-to-Asia routes, while ExpressVPN dropped to 84%.

Both are dramatically faster than the competition, but NordVPN's WireGuard-based NordLynx has a measurable edge for gaming, torrenting, and large file transfers.

Winner: NordVPN 🏆

Privacy & Security: A Close Call

Both providers are headquartered in privacy-friendly jurisdictions — NordVPN in Panama, ExpressVPN in the British Virgin Islands. Both have undergone multiple independent no-logs audits and offer AES-256 encryption, perfect forward secrecy, and DNS leak protection. But NordVPN's 2026 scale now matters too: the service spans 9,000+ servers in 130+ countries, which gives it more flexibility for location choice, congestion avoidance, and faster regional matching.

Where NordVPN pulls ahead is with Threat Protection Pro, a built-in feature that blocks malicious websites, trackers, and ads at the network level — essentially a lightweight antivirus. NordVPN also offers Double VPN (routing through two servers) and Onion Over VPN for Tor integration.

ExpressVPN counters with its TrustedServer technology — RAM-only servers that wipe all data on reboot, making it physically impossible to store logs. This is an elegant security approach, though NordVPN's RAM-only servers have caught up in 2026.

Winner: NordVPN (by a slim margin thanks to Threat Protection Pro)

Streaming: ExpressVPN Still King

If your primary use case is unblocking streaming services, ExpressVPN remains the gold standard. In our tests, it unblocked Netflix in all 20 regions tested, plus Hulu, BBC iPlayer, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, and several niche regional services — all on the first try.

NordVPN is also excellent for streaming (it unblocked Netflix in 18/20 regions), but we encountered occasional hiccups with BBC iPlayer and DAZN that required switching servers. ExpressVPN's MediaStreamer DNS feature gives it a unique advantage for smart TVs and consoles that don't natively support VPN apps.

Winner: ExpressVPN 🏆

Ease of Use

ExpressVPN's apps are beautifully minimal — one big connect button, a clean server list, and smart location suggestions. It's the VPN we recommend to anyone who just wants to connect and forget.

NordVPN's apps are more feature-rich, which comes with a slight learning curve. The map-based interface on mobile can feel cluttered, though the 2026 redesign significantly improved navigation. Power users will appreciate the granular controls; casual users may prefer ExpressVPN's simplicity.

Winner: ExpressVPN (for simplicity) / NordVPN (for power users)

Pricing: NordVPN Wins Big

This is where NordVPN has a decisive advantage:

  • NordVPN 2-year plan: ~$3.50/month ($83.76 total)
  • ExpressVPN 2-year plan: ~$6.67/month ($160 total)

NordVPN is nearly half the price on long-term plans while offering more features. ExpressVPN justifies its premium with superior streaming and simplicity, but for most users, NordVPN's value proposition is hard to beat.

Winner: NordVPN 🏆
