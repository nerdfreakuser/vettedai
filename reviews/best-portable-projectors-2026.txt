<h2>Buying Guide: How to Choose a Portable Projector</h2>
<p>When shopping for a portable projector in 2026, keep these crucial factors in mind:</p>
<h3>1. Brightness (ANSI or ISO Lumens)</h3>
Brightness is the most critical spec for any projector. Anything under 300 lumens will require a pitch-black room. Between 400-800 lumens, you can handle a little ambient light. Beware of cheap projectors claiming "10,000 lumens"—look specifically for <strong>ANSI</strong> or <strong>ISO</strong> lumen measurements, which are standardized.
<h3>2. Battery Life vs. Power Banks</h3>
Do you need an internal battery, or are you okay bringing a high-capacity power bank? Projectors with internal batteries (like the XGIMI Halo+) are incredibly convenient but heavier. Models like the LG CineBeam Q ditch the battery to keep the size down and image quality up, but require an external power source.
<h3>3. Smart OS and App Support</h3>
A portable projector isn't very portable if you also have to carry a Roku or Apple TV stick. Look for projectors with built-in Google TV, Android TV, webOS, or Tizen. <strong>Important Note:</strong> Many projectors do not have official Netflix certification, forcing you to use clunky web-based workarounds. If Netflix is your primary app, ensure the projector supports it natively (like the Nebula Mars 3 Air or LG CineBeam Q).
