Gaming in 2026 comes with a new set of demands: faster servers, global matchmaking, day-one regional releases, and ever-present DDoS threats for streamers and competitive players. Why Gamers Need a VPN in 2026

VPNs have gone from a privacy niche to a mainstream gaming tool for three key reasons:

  • DDoS protection: Competitive and streaming gamers are increasingly targeted. A VPN masks your real IP, making DDoS attacks ineffective.
  • ISP throttling bypass: Many ISPs deliberately throttle gaming and streaming traffic during peak hours. A VPN encrypts traffic so your ISP can't selectively slow it down.
  • Early access to game releases: Connect to a New Zealand or Australian server to play global releases hours before your region's launch.
  • Access geo-locked content: Certain DLC, battle passes, and game stores are region-restricted. A VPN unlocks them instantly.
  • Safer public Wi-Fi gaming: Hotel or LAN party networks expose you to snooping — a VPN keeps your connection encrypted.

How We Tested: Gaming VPN Criteria

We evaluated each VPN across five criteria specific to gaming:

  • Latency (ping) overhead — measured baseline vs VPN ping to game servers in 3 regions
  • Download/upload speed — tested on 1 Gbps fibre connection via Speedtest CLI
  • DDoS protection — tested real IP exposure with WebRTC leak tests and under simulated flood conditions
  • Console compatibility — rated ease of PS5/Xbox router setup
  • Server coverage — number of countries and proximity to major game server regions (US East, EU West, Asia Pacific)

Can a VPN Actually Reduce Ping?

In specific scenarios, yes. If your ISP is throttling gaming traffic (common during peak hours), a VPN bypasses that throttling by encrypting your data so the ISP can't classify it as gaming traffic. If you're routing through a congested path to a game server, a VPN might find a cleaner route. We saw ping reductions of 15–30ms on EU-based game servers when using NordVPN to bypass throttling on UK broadband during peak hours.

However, if your ISP isn't throttling and your route is already optimal, a VPN will add a small overhead (typically 5–15ms). The key is testing — most of our top picks offer a 30-day money-back guarantee, so you can verify the improvement on your own connection before committing.

VPN Setup for Gaming Consoles (PS5, Xbox Series X)

Since PS5 and Xbox don't support native VPN apps, you have two options:

  • Router method (recommended): Flash your router with VPN firmware (NordVPN and ExpressVPN both have guides for popular routers like Asus RT-AX88U and Netgear Nighthawk). All devices on your network get VPN coverage automatically.
  • PC hotspot method: Connect your PC to VPN, then share your PC's internet connection as a Wi-Fi hotspot. Connect your console to that hotspot. Free but less convenient.

ExpressVPN's Aircove removes both complexities — it's a purpose-built VPN router that handles everything out of the box.
