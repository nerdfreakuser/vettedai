Best Smart Rings in 2026: Top Picks for Sleep, Fitness & Health Tracking

Looking for the perfect wearable without the screen? We review the top smart rings of 2026, comparing Oura, Samsung, RingConn, and Ultrahuman.

Smartwatches are great, but sometimes you just don't want another screen glowing on your wrist. Enter the smart ring. Over the last few years, smart rings have transformed from niche novelties into incredibly powerful holistic health trackers. They monitor your heart rate, blood oxygen levels, sleep stages, temperature trends, and daily stress—all from a sleek, unobtrusive titanium band on your finger.

In 2026, the smart ring market has exploded with incredible options. While Oura once held a monopoly on this form factor, tech giants like Samsung and rising stars like RingConn and Ultrahuman have entered the fray, pushing battery life further, improving sensor accuracy, and—crucially—eliminating the dreaded monthly subscription fee.

We've spent weeks testing and comparing the latest generation of smart rings, poring over sleep data, workout recovery insights, and overall comfort to bring you the definitive guide to the best smart rings available right now. Let's dive in.

1. Oura Ring Gen 4: The Best Overall Holistic Health Tracker

Oura virtually invented the modern smart ring category, and their 4th Generation model continues to set the gold standard for accuracy and actionable insights.

Price: $349 (Plus $5.99/month subscription)

The Oura Ring Gen 4 features aerospace-grade titanium, impressive durability, and a completely smooth interior, eliminating the subtle sensor bumps found on older models. Oura’s companion app is unparalleled when it comes to translating raw data into digestible daily scores for Readiness, Sleep, and Activity. The new algorithmic updates are particularly impressive, detecting naps flawlessly and offering nuanced stress tracking throughout the day.

Pros

  • Industry-Leading Sleep Tracking: The most accurate sleep staging algorithms we've tested outside of a clinical lab.
  • Sleek, Comfortable Design: The fully smooth interior is a game-changer for all-day wearability.
Actionable Data: The Oura app excels at telling you what* your data means, not just presenting numbers.

Cons

  • Subscription Required: You must pay an ongoing monthly fee to access the majority of your data.
  • Scratches over time: The darker finishes tend to show wear and tear after a few months of heavy use.

2. Samsung Galaxy Ring: The Best Smart Ring for Android Users

Samsung made a massive splash when they finally launched their much-anticipated Galaxy Ring. For anyone firmly entrenched in the Samsung ecosystem, this is a no-brainer.

Price: $399 (No subscription required)

The standout feature of the Samsung Galaxy Ring isn't just its sleek, concave design (which helps prevent scratches)—it's the absolute lack of a subscription fee. You pay $399 upfront, and you have access to everything forever. The Galaxy Ring integrates perfectly with Samsung Health, pulling in data to give you an incredibly comprehensive "Energy Score" each morning based on your sleep, HRV, and previous day's activity.

Pros

  • Zero Subscription Fees: Your upfront cost is your final cost.
  • Incredible Battery Life: With the included charging case (which looks like a premium earbuds case), battery life extends beautifully.
  • Concave Design: Helps protect the exterior from daily scuffs and scratches.

Cons

  • Android/Samsung Focused: While technically usable with other Android phones, it shines brightest when paired with a Galaxy device. No iOS support.
  • Higher Upfront Price: Slightly more expensive out-of-the-box than competitors.

3. RingConn Gen 2: The Best Value and Battery Life

If you want top-tier tracking without the massive price tag or a subscription, the RingConn Gen 2 is the sleeper hit of the year.

Price: $299 (No subscription required)

RingConn surprised us by offering one of the slimmest, lightest, and longest-lasting smart rings on the market. The Gen 2 model boasts an astonishing 10-12 days of battery life on a single charge—nearly double that of the Oura and Samsung rings. Despite its lower price, the RingConn Gen 2 offers incredibly detailed sleep metrics, continuous heart rate monitoring, and blood oxygen tracking.

Pros

  • Unbeatable Battery Life: Easily goes a week and a half without needing a charge.
  • Budget-Friendly: At under $300 with zero subscription fees, it's the best value available.
  • Incredibly Slim: Noticeably lighter and less bulky than its predecessors.

Cons

  • App UI is Cluttered: The companion app provides tons of data but lacks the intuitive polish of Oura or Samsung.
  • Limited Integration: Doesn't tie into third-party fitness apps quite as seamlessly.

4. Ultrahuman Ring Air: The Best for Fitness Enthusiasts

Ultrahuman has carved out a niche by catering heavily to fitness buffs, biohackers, and those who want deep dives into their metabolic health.

Price: $349 (No subscription required)

The Ultrahuman Ring Air earns its name by being unbelievably lightweight. It feels like practically nothing on your finger. What sets Ultrahuman apart is their app's focus on actionable lifestyle changes. It gives you "stimulant windows" indicating the best times to drink coffee, circadian rhythm insights, and deep integrations with continuous glucose monitors (CGMs) if you choose to wear one.

Pros

  • Biohacker's Dream: Incredible, nuanced data regarding metabolism, recovery, and circadian rhythms.
  • Lightweight Comfort: One of the most comfortable rings to wear during heavy weightlifting.
  • No Subscription: A massive plus for the sheer volume of data you receive.

Cons

  • Overwhelming Data: Casual users might find the sheer volume of metrics and "zones" confusing.
  • Sleep Tracking Trails Oura: While good, it isn't quite as razor-sharp at detecting precise sleep stages as the Oura 4.

The Verdict: Our Top Pick

Choosing the best smart ring in 2026 ultimately comes down to how much you hate subscriptions and what ecosystem you currently live in.

If you want the absolute best app experience, the most refined insights, and the most accurate sleep data on the market, the Oura Ring Gen 4 remains the reigning champion. Yes, the subscription is annoying, but Oura's software polish is currently unmatched.

However, if you are an Android user and absolutely refuse to pay monthly fees, the Samsung Galaxy Ring is a triumphant piece of hardware that acts as a true set-and-forget fitness tracker. And for the budget-conscious, you simply cannot beat the value proposition of the RingConn Gen 2.

No matter which you choose, dropping the screen in favor of a smart ring is one of the best upgrades you can make to your daily wellness routine.
