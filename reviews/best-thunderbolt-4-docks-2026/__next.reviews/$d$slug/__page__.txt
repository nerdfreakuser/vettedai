For users who split their time between a home office and a studio, the Thunderbolt Go Dock is a lifesaver. You can easily throw it in a backpack without hauling a heavy power adapter. Its built-in power supply also makes cable management significantly easier for clean desk enthusiasts.

<h2>How to Choose the Right Thunderbolt Dock in 2026</h2>
<p>When shopping for a dock, keep these factors in mind:</p>
<ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-4"><li class="list-decimal"> <strong>Host Device Compatibility:</strong> While Thunderbolt 4 and 5 are generally universally compatible via USB-C, Mac users should be aware that standard M1/M2/M3 chips (non-Pro/Max versions) only support a single external display natively. A dock will not bypass this hardware limitation without DisplayLink workarounds.</li>
<li class="list-decimal"> <strong>Downstream Thunderbolt Ports vs. Display Outputs:</strong> Docks like the CalDigit TS5 and Razer Chroma offer multiple downstream Thunderbolt ports, which is great if you use USB-C monitors or fast external NVMe drives. Docks like the Anker 778 offer native HDMI/DisplayPort, which is better if you use standard monitors and want to avoid buying dongles.</li>
<li class="list-decimal"> <strong>Power Delivery:</strong> Check your laptop's original charger. If it requires 100W+, ensure the dock can provide at least 90W to avoid slow battery drain under heavy load.</li>
</ol>
